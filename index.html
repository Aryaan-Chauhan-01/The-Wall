Core Summit Directives

  • 1

    Establishment of "Bharat AI": A consortium of public-private partnerships focused on developing foundational models trained on highly diverse, multilingual Indian datasets to prevent cultural bias.

  • 2

    Mandatory Watermarking: All synthetic media generated by commercial APIs must now carry cryptographic watermarks to combat deepfakes ahead of the upcoming electoral cycles.

  • 3

    Green AI Mandate: Data centers expanding AI clusters must source a minimum of 60% of their energy from renewable sources to mitigate environmental impact.

The 'Yotta' Initiative

A $3 Billion public fund launched to subsidize local manufacturing of AI accelerators and tensor processing units, reducing reliance on foreign chip imports by 40% by 2029.

Cooling Innovations

Mandatory adoption of closed-loop liquid cooling systems for all new Tier 4 data centers dedicated to AI workloads to conserve water resources in arid regions.

The Bharat AI Ethics Framework

Technological advancement without guardrails presents severe risks. The Summit finalized the 'Bharat AI Framework', a set of binding guidelines for enterprise AI. This section organizes these policies into four core pillars. Hover over each pillar to understand the regulatory mechanisms put in place.
