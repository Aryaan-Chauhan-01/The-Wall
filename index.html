Urgent Safety Advisory: Jan 2026

Scammers have evolved. We have detected a massive spike in "Deepfake Interview" scams and "Hardware Security Deposit" frauds this month. The desperation for new year jobs is being exploited.

1. The "Deepfake HR" Scam

High Severity

Scammers use AI video generation to impersonate HR managers from top MNCs (TCS, Google, etc.) on video calls. They conduct a convincing "interview."

The Trap: They ask you to transfer ₹5,000 - ₹25,000 immediately for a "Background Verification Code" or "Laptop Insurance."

Prevalent on: Telegram, WhatsApp Video

2. The "Task-Based" Ponzi

Medium Severity

"Work from home - Rate movie trailers." You get paid small amounts initially to build trust. Then, you are asked to "invest" to unlock "VIP Tasks."

The Trap: Once you deposit a large sum (e.g., ₹50k), the account is locked, and the "recruiter" blocks you.

Prevalent on: Instagram Ads, Telegram Groups

3. Fake Visa Processing

Critical

Targeting job seekers looking for opportunities in Europe/Canada. Fake agencies issue realistic-looking "Offer Letters" and "Visa Sponsorships."

The Trap: They demand fees for "Medical Tests" or "Embassy Slots" via UPI. Legitimate visa fees are never paid to personal UPI IDs.

Prevalent on: LinkedIn DMs, Email

4. "Paid Training" Promise

Widespread

Companies claiming to hire for "Junior Data Analyst" roles but requiring a mandatory 3-month paid training course (₹40k) with a "100% Job Guarantee."

The Trap: The training is worthless, and the "guaranteed" job doesn't exist or is an unpaid internship.

Prevalent on: Job Portals
